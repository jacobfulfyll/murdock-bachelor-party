# Soul

## What This Is

A private website for Christopher Murdock's bachelor party in Salt Lake City, July 16–19, 2026. Sixteen friends share one URL and one password. Everything they need for the trip lives here — where to be, what to bring, what to eat, how much it costs.

The site is a gift as much as a utility. It should feel special to land on, not like a Google Doc.

## Target Users

Sixteen guys attending the trip. They'll mostly open this on their phones between Thursday arrival and Sunday departure. They are not a technical audience. The site should require zero explanation.

## Core Experience

**The entrance.** `index.html` is a full-screen Utah mountain landscape with a password prompt. The clue is "Murdock's favorite instrument and animal combined as one word." Correct answers: `saxophonecat` or `saxcat`. On success, photos of his cat and saxophone explode across the screen before the page transitions to home.

**The home page.** Welcome message, Airbnb link, transportation notes (Sprinter van for the group), dates and location.

**What To Pack.** A curated packing list for a 4-day mountain/city/lake weekend. Split layout with a packing image on the right.

**Arrivals.** Arrival and departure schedule for all 16 attendees, presented as cards with flight/arrival times. Split layout with a Salt Lake City photo on the right.

**Groceries.** The full shopping list for 16 adults across 4 days — beer, liquor, mixers, food, house supplies. Every line item is voteable up or down (votes sync live across all devices via Firebase). A sticky suggestion form lets anyone propose a new item; suggestions appear in a "Group Suggestions" section and are also voteable.

**Schedule.** Four-day slideshow. Each day has three sections (morning, afternoon, evening), each section gets a full-screen split panel: info on one side, activity photo on the other. Panels alternate left-right. Navigation at the top lets you jump directly to any day; arrow keys also work.

**Costs.** Line-item cost breakdown by day and category — transport, activities, meals, groceries — with per-person totals.

## Aesthetic Intention

Nature-inspired, dark, slightly luxurious. Think a high-end outdoor resort site, not a party flyer. Deep pine greens as the base. Warm sandstone accents. Cream text. No confetti, no bright colors, no generic party decorations. The tone is "we planned something worth celebrating."

The only departure from the serious aesthetic is the photo explosion on entry — that moment is deliberately absurd and joyful, because it's Murd's cat.
